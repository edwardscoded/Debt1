What is DEBT1?

Issued by Global Debt Reserve (GDR)

DEBT1 is a next-generation digital asset that tracks the scale of global debt obligations, not fiat reserves.

📈

Index

DEBT1 references sovereign, corporate, and household debt worldwide (currently over $305 trillion).

📊

Mechanism

Unlike fiat-backed stablecoins, DEBT1 does not maintain a 1:1 peg to a single currency. Instead, it is designed to mirror the direction and magnitude of global debt growth.

🔍

Transparency

The debt index can be observed through on-chain reporting, data sources, and financial indicators on the Solana blockchain.

🎯

Utility

DEBT1 provides exposure to the underlying force of credit and obligations that drive the world economy — functioning as a thematic asset rather than a fiat replacement.

⚠️ Important Disclosure

DEBT1 is not a stablecoin.

It is a digital asset inspired by the concept of global debt, with no guarantee of price stability or redemption at par. Holders should view DEBT1 as an index-style thematic token rather than a substitute for stablecoins like USD1 or USDC.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about DEBT1.

What backs DEBT1? ▼
DEBT1 is not "backed" in the traditional sense. It is a thematic digital asset that tracks the scale of global debt obligations (currently over $305 trillion). Unlike stablecoins, DEBT1 has no guarantee of price stability or redemption at par.
Is DEBT1 a stablecoin? ▼
No. DEBT1 is not a stablecoin and does not maintain a 1:1 peg to any currency. It is designed to mirror the direction and magnitude of global debt growth, making it a thematic index token rather than a stable store of value.
How is this different from traditional stablecoins? ▼
Traditional stablecoins like USD1 provide stability through 1:1 pegs to dollars. DEBT1 is fundamentally different — it's an index-style thematic token that tracks global debt growth, not price stability. Think of it as exposure to the world's obligations rather than a stable store of value.
How does the index tracking work? ▼
DEBT1 is designed to mirror the direction and magnitude of global debt movements through on-chain data reporting. The token value may fluctuate based on debt growth patterns rather than maintaining price stability like traditional stablecoins.
Is DEBT1 audited? ▼
Yes, DEBT1 smart contracts undergo regular third-party audits. All audit reports, reserve proofs, and transparency documents are publicly available in our Transparency section.
How do I integrate DEBT1 into my protocol? ▼
Developers can integrate DEBT1 using standard SPL token contracts on Solana. Contact our integration team for technical documentation, API access, and partnership opportunities specific to the Solana ecosystem.
What are the risks? ▼
As with any digital asset, DEBT1 carries smart contract, oracle, and market risks. Additionally, there are Solana network-specific risks to consider. Please read our full risk disclosures and only invest what you can afford to lose. Past performance does not guarantee future results.
Why Solana? ▼
Solana's high throughput, low transaction costs, and fast finality make it ideal for DEBT1's frequent peg adjustments and real-time debt index tracking. The network's scalability ensures smooth operations even during high-volume periods.
